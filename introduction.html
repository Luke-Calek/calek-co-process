UX Maturity Scale

Within each organisation there will be different levels of UX knowledge and support. Depending on your situation, you may need to invest more time into maturing the view of various stakeholders to ensure UX is a vital to your product or service.

Defining Goals within UX

Success can only be achived, if success is define.

Instead of just creating for the sake of creating, outline a clear goal for what you, your team or your company wishes to achive with UX. The below toolkit is a simple first step is aligning on a vision and mission for UX.


Define a UX Strategy

Write a definition of the UX strategy for your workplace/organisation that includes all 5 of the elements below. Keep this short, 1-2 sentances maximum.

The Five Elements

  • Timing - when
  • Beneficiaries - who
  • Process - how
  • Value - what
  • Experience Design - ???

Examples

Enterprise example:
UX Strategy is a collaborative process at the inception of a new product, created to put our customers at the core of every decision we make to bring value to their retail business with a great user experience.
Agency example:
UX Strategy is the collaborative process of creating world class user experience with our clients to create or enhance great products and services for their business that exceed their business goals and solves their users needs.
Non-Profit example:
UX Strategy is the activities we pursue to create new services for underprivileged people to higher their chances to have a better life, while our donors feel empowered to enable services through products that are tailored to their needs.
