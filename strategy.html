Overview

UX Strategy is best described as creating the framework to find the right idea. As the frist diamond on this UX process, Strategy contains both divergant and convergant tasks.



Diverge: Explore


Define the Customer and Job to be Done

Customers buy or use products and services to get a job done. Customers are always looking for the most convenient method to complete the job and in most cases are not loyal to the method.

  • For example a job to be done is ‘listening to music' - first people used records, then tapes, CDs, iPods and now streaming services.

There are two overacting types of customers: WIP

  • The job executors 
    Example: I need to make a playlist and play music at a party
  • The job beneficiaries
    Example: I want to listen to music at a party

Discover Unmet Needs

From the previous list of needs you now need to find which are unmet by the current market. Quantitative research will help reveal how satisfied customer are - an unmet need is when it is important to a customer but not satisfied with the solutions available. WIP



Converge: Strategize


Find Segments of Opportunity

By sorting the unmet needs by themes you can start to create segments of users you can address with a UX solution.

User Needs Workshop
Introduces and familiarises user needs to all stakeholders, allowing for grouping and prioritisation.

Size the Market Opportunity

Quantify the number of people who execute the jobs you are supporting and what customers will pay to get the job done better.


Conduct Competitive Analysis

Using all the unmet needs, you can now accurately compare how much better or worse products in-market are at helping customers to get the job done.

Competitor Status
  • If there products in the market that don’t solve unmet user needs or are underdeveloped, your product / service can likely be launched as an MVP.
  • If there are well developed products that meet many user needs, your product / service shouldn’t be launched as an MVP. To take hold in an established market you should launch a MAP - Minimum Awesome Product.
