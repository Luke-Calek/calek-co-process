Stakeholder Interviews

Interviewing a range of people in and outside your team / organisation to understand the current ambitions, processes and blockers for your project. Addtionally, these interviews can help create a shared vision and earn trust and buy-in.

Discover:
  • Business goals – how will the design support business objectives?
  • User needs – how will the design help the users?
  • Technical limitations – what technical obstacles need to be overcome?

Journey Mapping

The aim of a journey map is to capture steps in a users journey to complete a task, along with insights into each steps' positive and negative elements. Typicaly the horizontal axis are steps in a linear or branching journey, and the vertical access is for themes and analysis.

Journey maps provide value by visually collecting and summarising a range of complex and numerious steps that can be easily shared with team members and stakeholders who need insights into a process but didn't not complete first-hand research.

Themes and Analysis
  • Actions – what the user needs to do to move to the next step in the journey
  • Question – things the user needs answered either before they move to the next step, or to reassure them when moving to the next step
  • Positive – enjoyable things that improve the experience
  • Negative – Poin points, frustrations or annoyances that detract from the experience
  • Opportunities – areas to explore in the future that address the above problems identified.

Competitor Analysis

Competitor analysis is best when it goes deeper than look and feel, to analysising if the competitor's product's features meet their user's needs.

Using user needs, found via User Research of Journey Mapping, you can accurately review in-market competitors and see how they help customers to get their job done.

Update and refine

Competitor Status

What your analysis of competitors reveals might hints to how you should best launch your product service:

  • If existing products in the market don’t solve user needs or are underdeveloped, your product or service can likely be launched as an MVP.
  • If there are well developed products that meet many user needs, your product or service shouldn’t be launched as an MVP. To take hold in an established market you should launch a MAP - Minimum Awesome Product.

User Personas

"We want archetypes, not stereotypes"

When created and used correctly, user personas can be great guides to the expectations of users. When used incorrectly they can be distracting and misleading by containing information not important to creation of an interface or experience.

Create personas with a focus on:
  • Pain point and needs
  • Techinical knowledge
  • Job-to-be-done, sub-tasks or workflows – enjoyable things that improve the experience

User Stories

As a [user group]
I need / want / expect to… [what does the user want to do?]
So that… [why does the user want to do this?]

‘User Stories are the needs that a user has of a product or service, and that a product or service must satisfy for the user to get the right outcome for their task.
